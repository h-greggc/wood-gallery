Edge grains for cutting boards

Edge grain is the thinner side of a board. Edge-grain cutting boards are sizeable “butcher block” styles of boards. Edge-grain building involves many glue joints (face-to-face) of edge grain strips. Each glue joint dramatically increases the strength of the board. Thus, edge-grain boards can be built to remain flat from small sizes to full kitchen counter tops.

Edge grains for cutting/charcuterie

Edge grain is the thinner side of a board. Edge-grain cutting boards are the “butcher block” style among cutting boards. Edge-grain building involves many glue joints (face-to-face) of edge grain strips of different hardwoods. Each glue joint increases the strength of the board and greatly improves the resistance to warping. Thus, edge-grain boards can be built to remain flat from small sizes “cheese boards” to full-size kitchen counter tops.
