## Grain Faces: three sides of a board

### Face grain

Cutting and charcuterie boards are usually designated as one of three grain appearances: _face_, _edge_, and _end_. Face grain is the widest side of the board, Face grains offer natural wood growth-ring patterns that can either high contrasting curved  arches (a.k.a. “cathedrals”), elipses, straight lines, curly or even “tiger”  striped figures.[^1] In wood working, a single, wide board showing face grain is usually the prettiest presentation of the wood. Some projects benefit artisically and structurally by having very straight face grain, while other projects favor art over durability for random patterns of face grain. 

## Edge grain

Edge grain is the narrower side of a flat board. Edge grain cutting boards are the most popular cutting board for home use because they are very stable, reliable and they can be artistically assembled from different species of harrd woods within the sam board. sizeable “butcher block” styles of boards. Edge-grain building involves many glue joints (face-to-face) of edge grain strips. Each glue joint dramatically increases the strength of the board. Thus, edge-grain boards can be built to remain flat from small sizes to full kitchen counter tops.
