## Grain Faces: three sides of a board

### Face grain

Cutting and charcuterie boards are usually designated as one of three grain appearances: _face_, _edge_, and _end_. Face grain is the widest side of the board, Face grains offer natural wood growth-ring patterns that can either high contrasting curved  arches (a.k.a. “cathedrals”), elipses, straight lines, curly or even “tiger”  striped figures.[^1] In wood working, a single, wide board showing face grain is usually the prettiest presentation of the wood. Some projects benefit both artistically and structurally by having a very straight face grain, while other projects favor art of a flowing, curvy face grain over durability.  

## Edge grain

Edge grain is the narrower side of a flat board. Edge grain cutting boards are the most popular cutting board for home use because they are very stable, solid and they can be artistic arrangements of different species of hardwoods within one board. Edge-grain building involves many glue joints (face-to-face) between edge grain strips. Each glue joint increases the strength of the board. Edge-grain boards can be sized from small cutting board to full-sized kitchen counter tops.

## End grain
